# 🌍 Humanitarian OSINT

> Applying open-source intelligence techniques to crisis response, disaster analysis, conflict monitoring, and humanitarian event verification — from fragmented early reports to a structured, evidence-backed situational intelligence assessment.

Every investigation in this folder starts with an unverified humanitarian event or crisis indicator and ends with a documented, reproducible intelligence finding. No assumptions. No shortcuts. Evidence in, intelligence out.

---

| Technique | What It Involves |

| **Open-Source Data Analysis** | Processing publicly available datasets to extract actionable humanitarian intelligence |
| **Evidence Documentation** | Recording all findings in structured, verifiable, and reproducible investigation reports |

## 🔬 Investigative Methodology

Each investigation follows a disciplined, repeatable eight-stage workflow:

```
╔══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╗
║               INVESTIGATIVE METHODOLOGY                      ║
╠══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╣
║                                                              ║
║  01  RESEARCH QUESTION                                       ║
║      Define the humanitarian event, crisis, or intelligence  ║
║      requirement under investigation                         ║
║                              ↓                               ║
║  02  COLLECTION                                              ║
║      Gather publicly available reports, satellite imagery,   ║
║      geospatial data, and open-source situational evidence   ║
║                              ↓                               ║
║  03  VERIFICATION                                            ║
║      Validate source credibility, information accuracy,      ║
║      and evidence integrity                                  ║
║                              ↓                               ║
║  04  CORRELATION                                             ║
║      Cross-reference findings across multiple independent    ║
║      sources, platforms, and humanitarian databases          ║
║                              ↓                               ║
║  05  ANALYSIS                                                ║
║      Apply geospatial, visual, and contextual analysis       ║
║      techniques to build a situational intelligence picture  ║
║                              ↓                               ║
║  06  FINDINGS                                                ║
║      Establish key conclusions on the event scope, impact,   ║
║      and humanitarian significance                           ║
║                              ↓                               ║
║  07  INTELLIGENCE ASSESSMENT                                 ║
║      Assess confidence level, information gaps, source       ║
║      limitations, and alternative interpretations            ║
║                              ↓                               ║
║  08  REPORT                                                  ║
║      Document conclusions in a structured, reproducible      ║
║      humanitarian intelligence report                        ║
║                                                              ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╝
```

Every conclusion is evidence-backed. Every step is reproducible.

## ⚠️ Responsible Use

All investigations in this folder are conducted strictly using open-source intelligence and publicly available data, in accordance with humanitarian OSINT ethical principles:

- **Legal compliance** — All methods comply with applicable laws and platform terms of service
- **Public data only** — No unauthorised access to restricted, classified, or confidential information
- **Do no harm** — Investigations prioritise the safety and dignity of affected populations at all times
- **Conflict sensitivity** — Findings involving active conflict zones are handled with particular care and discretion
- **Responsible handling** — Sensitive findings are documented responsibly and not used to cause harm
- **Educational purpose** — All case studies are conducted for learning and portfolio demonstration only
